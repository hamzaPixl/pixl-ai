# Vision Document Template

Slide-ready section format for advisory vision documents. Each section maps to 1-3 presentation slides. Sections are ordered for narrative flow — the audience should be convinced by the end.

## Document Structure

### Cover
- Company name + logo placeholder
- Vision title (e.g., "Vision 2030: The Citizen Digital Mailbox")
- Date and confidentiality notice
- Advisor attribution

### Section 1: Context & Ambition
**Key message**: Why this document exists and what's at stake.

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Current situation | Where the company stands today (1-2 sentences) |
| Market trigger | What's changing — regulation, technology, behavior |
| Ambition statement | What the company wants to become |
| Document scope | What this vision covers and what it doesn't |

### Section 2: The Paradigm Shift
**Key message**: The world is changing, and this company is positioned to lead.

Frame as a clear before/after:

| Today (FROM) | Tomorrow (TO) |
|---|---|
| [Current state description] | [Future state description] |
| [Pain point 1] | [Solution 1] |
| [Pain point 2] | [Solution 2] |
| [Pain point 3] | [Solution 3] |

Include: technology shifts, regulatory drivers, behavioral changes that make the "TO" state inevitable.

### Section 3: Value Proposition
**Key message**: Here is the problem we solve and why it matters.

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Problem** | The core problem, quantified (X million people affected, Y€ lost annually) |
| **Solution** | What the company will deliver (one paragraph) |
| **Strategic objective** | The measurable goal (market share, user count, revenue target) |
| **Beneficiaries** | Who benefits and how (citizens, businesses, government) |

### Section 4: Why This Company?
**Key message**: Nobody else can do this as well as we can.

List 5-7 competitive advantages, each as a titled bullet:

```
1. **[Advantage name]**: [1-2 sentence explanation with data point]
2. **[Advantage name]**: [1-2 sentence explanation with data point]
...
```

Each advantage should be:
- Specific to this company (not generic)
- Backed by evidence (client count, certification, market share)
- Difficult for competitors to replicate

### Section 5: Market Landscape
**Key message**: The market is large, growing, and driven by forced demand.

| Market | Size | CAGR | Key driver |
|--------|------|------|-----------|
| [Market 1] | €X.XB | X.X% | [Driver] |
| [Market 2] | €X.XB | X.X% | [Driver] |

Include: regulatory timeline (key dates), technology adoption curves, and geographic breakdown if relevant.

### Section 6: Competitive Positioning
**Key message**: Here is where we fit — and where competitors fall short.

Positioning matrix (table or 2x2 visual description):

| Competitor | Strength | Weakness | Our advantage |
|-----------|----------|----------|---------------|
| [Name] | [What they do well] | [Gap] | [How we differentiate] |

### Section 7: Sector Deep-Dives
**Key message**: These are the verticals where we'll win first.

For each sector (2-4 sectors), one sub-section:

#### Sector: [Name]

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Why this sector** | Strategic fit rationale |
| **Pain points** | 3-5 problems we solve |
| **Use cases** | 3-5 concrete product applications |
| **Competitive gap** | Why incumbents aren't solving this |
| **Volume** | Addressable market or user count |

Include a scoring summary table at the end:

| Sector | Volume | Regulatory | Emotional | Ease of Entry | Revenue | **Total** |
|--------|--------|-----------|-----------|---------------|---------|-----------|
| [Name] | X/5 | X/5 | X/5 | X/5 | X/5 | **XX/25** |

### Section 8: Functional Architecture
**Key message**: Here is what we'll build — structured for adoption and monetization.

#### Feature Tiers

| Tier | Purpose | Examples | Monetization |
|------|---------|----------|-------------|
| **Free** | Drive adoption | [3-4 features] | Free / ad-supported |
| **Premium** | Deliver value | [3-4 features] | Subscription |
| **Enterprise** | Serve organizations | [3-4 features] | Per-seat / custom |

#### Feature Categories

For each functional category:

**[Category Name]**
- [Feature 1]: [one-line description] — *[tier]*
- [Feature 2]: [one-line description] — *[tier]*
- [Feature 3]: [one-line description] — *[tier]*

### Section 9: Business Model
**Key message**: Here is how this vision generates revenue.

| Revenue Stream | Model | Year 1 | Year 3 | Year 5 |
|---------------|-------|--------|--------|--------|
| [Stream 1] | [Subscription/Transaction/...] | €X | €X | €X |
| [Stream 2] | [Model] | €X | €X | €X |
| **Total** | | **€X** | **€X** | **€X** |

Include: unit economics (CAC, LTV), network effects, and scalability levers.

### Section 10: Strategic Roadmap
**Key message**: Here is the path from today to the vision — phased, funded, and measurable.

#### Phase Overview

| Phase | Name | Duration | Objective | Investment |
|-------|------|----------|-----------|------------|
| 1 | [Name] | Q1-Q2 | [Goal] | €X / X FTEs |
| 2 | [Name] | Q3-Q4 | [Goal] | €X / X FTEs |
| 3 | [Name] | Year 2 | [Goal] | €X / X FTEs |
| 4 | [Name] | Year 3+ | [Goal] | €X / X FTEs |

#### Phase Detail (per phase)

**Phase X: [Name]** (Duration)
- **Milestones**: [3-5 measurable milestones]
- **KPIs**: [2-3 success metrics with targets]
- **Investment**: [Team, budget, infrastructure]
- **Dependencies**: [What must be true]
- **Deliverables**: [What gets shipped]

### Section 11: Go-to-Market
**Key message**: Here is how we acquire users and grow.

| Wave | Target | Channel | Lever | Timeline |
|------|--------|---------|-------|----------|
| 1 | [First movers] | [Channel] | [Why they'll adopt] | [When] |
| 2 | [Growth segment] | [Channel] | [Lever] | [When] |

### Section 12: Risk Analysis
**Key message**: We've identified the risks and have mitigations ready.

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| [Risk 1] | High/Med/Low | High/Med/Low | [Strategy] |
| [Risk 2] | ... | ... | ... |

### Section 13: Call to Action
**Key message**: Here is what we need to decide and invest — now.

- **Decision requested**: [What the board/leadership needs to approve]
- **Investment ask**: [Total amount for Phase 1]
- **Expected return**: [Revenue or strategic value by Year X]
- **Next steps**: [3-5 immediate actions with owners and deadlines]
- **Top 3 arguments**:
  1. [Market argument — forced demand, size, timing]
  2. [Competitive argument — unique positioning, moats]
  3. [Risk of inaction — what happens if they don't move]

## Formatting Guidelines

- **One key message per section**: Every section starts with a bolded "Key message" line
- **Tables over prose**: Use tables for structured data — they translate directly to slides
- **Data citations**: Every number has a source in parentheses — `(Source: [name], [year])`
- **Professional tone**: Authoritative but accessible — write for C-level and board audiences
- **Language consistency**: Entire document in one language (FR/EN/NL as specified)
- **Section independence**: Each section should be understandable on its own (for when slides are shared out of order)
